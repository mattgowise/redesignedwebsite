A flawless printed product starts with a perfectly prepared file. Explore our guides below to understand the fundamentals of commercial print, or use our interactive tools to plan your next project.

Whether you are submitting a corporate annual report, a heavily illustrated folklore zine, or a short run of bespoke merchandise, setting your artwork up correctly saves time and ensures the best possible result on the press.

Understanding Colour: CMYK vs. RGB

The most common issue in pre-press is the difference between how colours look on a screen and how they look on physical paper.

RGB (Red, Green, Blue)

This is the colour model used by digital screens (monitors, phones, TVs). Because screens emit light, RGB can produce incredibly bright, neon, and highly saturated colours. We cannot print directly from RGB files.

CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key/Black)

This is the standard colour model for commercial printing. Presses combine these four ink colours to reproduce images. Because ink absorbs light rather than emitting it, CMYK has a smaller "colour gamut" than RGB.

The GoWise Solution

If your illustrations or designs rely on vibrant tones that standard CMYK struggles to hit, our Fujifilm 5UPER COLOUR© Digital Press utilizes an expanded colour gamut to simulate vivid RGB and exact Pantone® spot colours with exceptional accuracy.

Digital vs. Lithographic Printing

Which is right for you? Choosing the right press depends entirely on your quantity, timeline, and quality requirements.

Digital Printing

Best for short to medium runs (typically 1 to 500 units).

Because there are no traditional printing plates to set up, digital is incredibly fast and cost-effective for smaller batches, limited-edition art prints, and variable data projects.

Lithographic Printing (Litho)

Best for high-volume commercial runs.

Litho uses wet ink and custom plates. The initial setup takes longer and costs more, but once the press is running, the cost-per-page drops drastically. Litho also allows us to use traditional Pantone® spot colours and print on much larger raw sheet sizes (such as 450x640mm) for maximum yield.

Essential Artwork Setup Checklist

To avoid delays and additional pre-press fees, please ensure your files meet the following criteria before submission.

✓

Resolution

All images and raster graphics must be at least 300 DPI (Dots Per Inch) at their actual printed size.

✓

Bleed

If your design features colour or images that run right to the edge of the page, you must include a 3mm bleed on all sides. This gives our finishing team the tolerance needed to trim your pages perfectly without leaving white borders.

✓

Safe Zone

Keep all important text and logos at least 5mm away from the trim edge to ensure nothing gets cut off during the binding or guillotining process.

✓

File Formats

We strongly prefer Print-Ready PDFs. Please ensure all fonts are outlined or embedded, and transparency layers are flattened.
